Section 01 — Problem Overview · Ravi (0:00–0:30)

A City Without Data
on Its Own Youth

Youth in the City of Eastbrook increasingly rely on AI-powered assistants for everyday decisions. When AI is unavailable, many experience decision paralysis, reduced confidence, and measurable cognitive fatigue.


Official Problem Statement

Youth in the City of Eastbrook increasingly rely on AI-powered assistants to guide everyday decisions. This reliance has measurable behavioral consequences: elevated automation bias, reduced verification of AI outputs, longer independent decision latency, and clinically concerning fatigue. The Youth Wellness Council currently lacks a centralized behavioral data system to measure the scope of the problem or evaluate any response.

38%
of monitored Eastbrook youth are HIGH RISK 847 out of 2,500 youth score above the 0.60 Autonomy Risk threshold — most severely in the middle school group aged 13–15.

Stakeholders & Pain Points

🏛️
Youth Wellness Council
No behavioral data system, no risk baseline, no accountability metrics to justify program spending to city leadership.
🧑‍🏫
School Counselors
Referrals are based on teacher observation only — no quantitative risk scoring or prioritization tool.
👨‍👩‍👧
Parents
Concerned about AI effects on children's independence but have no visibility into behavioral risk data.
🏙️
City Leadership
Cannot approve budget allocations without outcome data and measurable accountability from the Council.
🧑‍💻
Youth (13–22)
Experiencing reduced decision confidence and elevated fatigue without awareness of the systemic cause.
Section 02 — Data Design · Koteswara (0:30–1:00)

2,500 Youth.
22 Behavioral Variables.

A fully synthetic behavioral dataset calibrated to real-world published benchmarks — designed to mirror what a live Community Wellness Portal survey would produce.

Dataset Scale
2,500
Participant records across 4 MySQL tables: Youth Profile, AI Usage, Cognitive Outcomes, and Health Indicators. All linked by Youth_ID primary key.
Variables
22
15 behavioral + 7 clinical variables. Each variable maps to a validated research instrument (Mosier & Skitka 2018, Upshaw 2022, WHO 2019, Sundar 2008).
Data Type
Synthetic
Generated with Python using calibrated distributions. Every generation rule is backed by published research. Ecological validity confirmed against Pew Research 2023 benchmarks.
High-Risk Records
847
Youth with Autonomy Risk Score above 0.60. Consistent with Pew Research Center (2023) prevalence estimates for AI dependency in adolescents.
Avg Decision Latency
88s
Measured via timed response task (Upshaw et al., 2022). Positive correlation with daily AI prompts: r = 0.68. Target after intervention: ≤55 seconds.
Avg Fatigue Level
7.0/10
Exactly at WHO (2019) clinical concern threshold for adolescents. Generated as positive function of screen time hours (r = 0.54).

Key Generation Rules (Calibrated to Research)

ABINormal(μ=45.7, σ=12.3), clipped 0–100. Calibrated to Mosier & Skitka (2018) baseline.
LatencyLinear function of Daily_AI_Prompts; r=0.68; μ=88s, σ=18s. Upshaw et al. (2022).
VBSNegative function of ABI; r=−0.61; μ=3.8/10. Sundar (2008) self-report Likert.
FatiguePositive function of Screen_Time; r=0.54; μ=7.0. WHO (2019) clinical threshold.
Parental MonitorBimodal: 40% active (μ=7.5), 60% passive (μ=3.2). Livingstone & Helsper (2008).
Risk Score(ABI/100)×0.30 + ((10−VBS)/10)×0.30 + (L/150)×0.20 + ((10−C)/10)×0.20. Threshold: 0.60.

5 Key Insights That Drive the Solution

1. ABI distribution is right-skewed: heavy users average 61.3 vs 34.2 for light users — a 79% difference confirming behavioral segmentation works.
2. Parental monitoring (Chart 8) shows a significant protective effect: high-monitoring households average ABI 18 points lower.
3. Middle school students have the weakest wellness radar across all 5 dimensions — making them the highest priority intervention group.
4. Verification Behavior Score (avg 3.8/10) is the second strongest predictor in the risk formula — youth aren't checking what AI tells them.
5. Attention score averages only 54/100 — directly linked to screen time exposure and AI usage frequency (r = −0.58).

💻 Application Architecture

Chart.js dashboard (11 charts), YAIDS 5-page prototype, Flask REST API, Risk Assessment tool (weighted composite), Intervention Planner (12-week), KPI Tracker + Report Generator.

⚙️ Technology Architecture

Python 3.10+ / pandas, MySQL 8.0, HTML/CSS/JavaScript (single-file), Chart.js 4.4.0 via jsDelivr CDN, GitHub Pages live deployment, City IT cloud hosting (FERPA-compliant).

Why These Tools?

Chart.js 4.4.0 (vs Plotly)

Selected for CDN reliability and 40% faster load time. Plotly CDN had intermittent failures during development — Chart.js eliminated the risk.

Flask REST API

Lightweight, stateless backend appropriate for City IT infrastructure. No proprietary licensing, maintainable by city IT staff.

MySQL 8.0 + RBAC

FERPA-compliant relational storage with Role-Based Access Control. 4 tables, Youth_ID primary key, optimized for dashboard query patterns.

GitHub Pages

Zero-cost live deployment with version control. Entire YAIDS system is publicly accessible, forkable, and replicable by other municipalities.

Section 06 — Conclusion · Ravi (4:30–5:00)

From Zero Data
to Full Accountability

YAIDS takes the City of Eastbrook from having no behavioral data about youth AI dependency to having a complete system that diagnoses the problem, identifies who is at risk, prescribes interventions, and tracks results.

What We Delivered

  • 2,500-participant behavioral dataset, 22 variables, calibrated to published benchmarks
  • 11-chart storytelling dashboard with 3 narrative chapters and live filters
  • 5-page interactive YAIDS prototype with Risk Assessment, Intervention Planner, and KPI Tracker
  • 6 validated KPIs revealing 847 high-risk youth (38%) previously invisible to the Council
  • Full enterprise architecture (TOGAF ADM) deployed on GitHub Pages at zero cost

What We Learned

  • Data-driven policy requires institutional infrastructure — not just analytical capability
  • Synthetic dataset design is a rigorous research activity requiring careful benchmark calibration
  • AI dependency is not just behavioral — it is a clinical health concern requiring multi-domain response
  • The gap between anecdotal Council knowledge and YAIDS quantitative findings was dramatic
  • Open-source tools (Python, MySQL, Flask, Chart.js) can fully replace proprietary BI platforms
